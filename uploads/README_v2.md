## The Campaign — "Hvorfor sagde jeg ik' noget?"

**Tagline:** Det svære er ofte ikke kun at have det svært — det svære er at få sagt det højt.

**Translation:** The hard part is often not just having a hard time — the hard part is saying it out loud.

### What It Is
A campaign consisting of a podcast series and a social media effort, giving young Danes and their close ones the courage and language to have the difficult conversation — both as the person struggling, and as the person who wants to ask but doesn't dare.

### The Core Problem We Address
Young people in Denmark know they are struggling. They've seen the videos. They know the symptoms. But they still haven't said anything out loud — to a friend, a parent, a partner. The barriers are:
- Shame
- Fear of being a burden
- Fear of making things worse
- No language for what they feel
- Belief that silence is protective

Social media gives young people information about what is wrong with them, but does not move them into real life. We want to move people into a real conversation. Action happens in the relationship, not in front of a screen.

### The Campaign Is for Both Sides
This is not only about the person struggling. It is equally about the person who wants to ask but hesitates — the friend, the sibling, the parent, the partner. We address both.

## Statistics on Danish Youth Mental Health

Use these to support the problem slide:
- **33%** of young people (16–24) rarely or never talk to their parents about setbacks and sorrows. (Psykiatrifonden, 2024)
- **56%** of young people (16–29) have needed support in the past year because they were not doing well. Of those, 29% sought help — 27% did not. (DUF, Ungeanalysen, 2025)
- **51%** of 9th grade students with no one to talk to have self-harmed. Among those who did have someone to talk to, the figure was 19% — self-harm was more than three times as prevalent among young people with no one to confide in. (Børns Vilkår, 2021)
- The problem is not only the scale — it is the silence. Young people, especially, adjust their behaviour to avoid burdening parents and close ones, and carry their struggles alone.

- Primary accent: Teal #028090
